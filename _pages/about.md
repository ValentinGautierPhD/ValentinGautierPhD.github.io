Currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at LS2N (Nantes, France), I specialize in medical imaging and inverse problems, with a strong focus on AI-driven approaches.

I hold a PhD from CREATIS (INSA Lyon) and an engineering degree from ENSIMAG (Grenoble, France). My career is driven by a dual passion: medical imaging research and sharing knowledge through teaching. Having completed more than 100 hours of mathematics instruction at INSA Lyon, I am now focused on securing a permanent faculty position to continue this synergy between research and education.

Beyond research, I am deeply committed to science outreach and bridging the gap between complex research and the general public. I regularly participate in public engagement initiatives, including talks at local high schools and international festivals like Pint of Science. In 2023, I was honored to receive the First Jury Prize at the Lyon/Saint-Étienne regional final of MT180 (the French edition of the Three Minute Thesis competition). You can watch my presentation on multimodal medical imaging [here](https://youtu.be/Qc-tK54tczc?si=LwwH6eHwcnlRis10).
